1 [1/10] Vocabulary

1.1 TODO corpulent

1.1.1 General

  • adj. excessively fat

1.1.2 Usage

  • Her father is too corpulent to play handball.

1.1.3 Story

  • corp- = body
  • -ent = being adj.

1.2 NEXT aerosol

1.2.1 General

  • n. a cloud of solid or liquid particles in a gas
  • adj. sth about n. meaning

1.2.2 Usage

  • an aerosol can.
  • They sprayed aerosol insect repellent into the faces of police.

1.2.3 Story

  • aero- = air
  • sol[uv(ut)] = loosen

1.3 DONE reflective

1.3.1 General

  • adj. think carefully | can reflect sth
  • adv. think before you leap!
  • n.

1.3.2 Usage

  • reflective glass
  • a quiet, reflective, astute man.

1.4 TODO submerge

1.4.1 General

  • v. sink below the surface, under water
  • vt. cover completely

1.4.2 Usage

  • submerged leaves
  • be submerged in the mighty torrent of history.
  • Who has submerged the fireplace with coom?

1.4.3 Note

immerse being covered completely in water
dip put into water temporarily
duck put one's head into water
plunge expeditiously
submerge deep into water for a long time

1.5 TODO tow

1.5.1 General

  • n. the act of hauling sth by means of a hitch or rope
  • v. drag behind

1.5.2 Usage

  • The truck used a cable to tow the car.
  • I can give you a tow if you want.

1.5.3 Note

drag  
draw drag to the drawing person
pull most general
haul with force, not related to direction, mostly used in voyage
tug drag from time to time, may not move at all
tow drag with tools
jerk drag quickly and suddenly

1.6 TODO perspective

1.6.1 General

  • n.
    • a way of regarding situations or topics etc
    • the appearance of things relative to one another as determined by their distance from the viewer

1.6.2 Usage

  • Try to see the issue from a different perspective.
  • We may get a clear perspective of the people's happy lives.

1.7 TODO flock

1.7.1 General

  • n.
    • a group of sth
    • small pieces of soft material used for filling cushions , chairs, etc.
  • vi. group together
  • vt. fill with sth

1.7.2 Usage

  • a flock of sheep
  • Birds of a feather flock together
  • flock dusted paper

1.8 TODO guise

1.8.1 General

  • n. a way in which sb/sth appears, often in a way that is different from usual or that hides the truth about them/it

1.8.2 Usage

  • They got into the school in the guise of inspectors
  • under the guise of friendship.

1.9 TODO slump

1.9.1 General

  • vi.
    • to fall in price, value, number, etc., suddenly and by a large amount
    • to sit or fall down heavily
  • n. a long time of state characterized by low spirit

1.9.2 Usage

  • Sales have slumped this year.
  • The old man slumped down in his chair.
